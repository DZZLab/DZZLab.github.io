Final Iteration

The Ogi Divider is my project as part of the Art of Making class at the University of Pittsburgh. The Ogi Divider is Japanese Ogi(æ‰‡)-fan inspired solution to inefficient study-space utilization.

Over the course of 3 weeks, we collected 62 survey responses and conducted 11 interviews, as well including expert interviews from 2 librarians. From these responses, we found that there was a high demand for privacy, comfort, and outlets.

With our these factors, engineered 3 "pretotypes" to user-test over the course of the next week.

From user-testing, 88% of users chose my Ogi-fan design for its intuitiveness and effective utility from the fan inspired design. It provided an unintrusive addition to current study spaces with flexible aesthetics for interior design - as well as the additional outlets at the base.
